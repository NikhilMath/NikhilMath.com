Books

    I believe you can really get to know me by the books I read. I try and mainly read self help books and non-fiction books. My goal is to finish one self help book every other week. 26 books in total for the year. I am about to be finished with book #25, "Retire Earlt with Real Estate".

    These are the books I have read in 2020, a breif description on what they are about, and If I recommend reading them or not.

  • "Thinking in Bets"
    Annie Duke

    This book teaches you how to look at decsions as statistical bets.
    Do Not Recommend
  • "Automatic Millionaire"
    David Bach

    The lesson in the book is that investing, saving, and paying off debt should be automatic and should not complicated. You should set it and forget it.
    Recommend
  • "Outliers"
    Malcolm Gladwell

    Outliers is an informaiton book about why great people are great, spoiler alert, it is because they work long hours with their craft at lucky times.
    Do Not Recommend
  • "The Tipping Point"
    Malcolm Gladwell

    Another information book.Iit is all about how social epidemics get started and how they pass their tipping point from being niche to something everyone knows about.
    Do Not Recommend
  • "Indistractable"
    Nir Eyal

    The world is designed to distract you. Marketing degrees are programs that teach people specifically how to distract the general population. This book teaches you how to avoid that.
    Do Not Recommend
  • "Atomic Habits"
    James Clear

    If you got 1% better everyday, you would be 37x better at the end of the year. Reading this you will learn how to build atomic habits that will make your 1% better everyday.
    Highly Recommend
  • "Can't Hurt Me"
    David Groggins

    David Groggins is a Navy Seal. He believes he has mastered self-discipline. He knows how to do what his mind wants him to do, and not what his body wants him to do. This book is a book on how he practices self-discipline.
    Recommend
  • "UltraLearning"
    Scott Young

    Ever wanted to learn how to learn a new skill in a short amount of time. This book will teach you how to "UltraLearn" any new skill you want.
    Highly Recommend
  • "So Good They Can't Ignore You"
    Cal Newport

    If you are so good at your trade, you will eventually be so good, no job could ignore you. This book teaches you that you can be so good they cannot ignore you in almost any skill. Do not follow your passion, instead, create your craft and develope it.
    Highly Recommend
  • "The Sleep Revolution"
    Arianna Huffington

    You don't have to be a scientist to know that sleep is good for you. Everyone knows they are at the top of their game when they have a full deep night's sleep behind them. Arianna teaches you how to have better sleep hygiene and why it is so important.
    Highly Recommend
  • "High Performing Habits"
    Brendon Burchard

    There are 6 habits that all high performers have. This book tells you what the 6 habits are and how to sharpen those skills.
    Do Not Recommend
  • "Deep Work"
    Cal Newport

    Deep Work is when you are completely imersed in what you are working on, having zero distractions, and being 100% focused. Mastering Deep Work will not only help you finish your tasks faster, but will make you love your work more. This book teaches you how to hit Deep Work.
    Highly Recommend
  • "The Untethered Soul"
    Michael Singer

    Do you know what your gut feeling is? If you do not know, that is normal. By reading The Untethered Soul, you will learn how to read yourself more.
    Do Not Recommend
  • "Who Stole My Pension"
    Robert Kiyosaki & Edward Siedle

    Did you know that the upcoming generations in America are haivng troubles with retirement financially? Most likely you do because of how common it is. Who stole your pension won't teach you how to be better at retirement, but it will let you know why you are most likely underpreparred for it.
  • "Thrive"
    Arianna Huffington

    Everyone knows about the two columns of life, money and power, but do you know about the third one? Thrive? Thrive is a measurement in life where life is enjoyable and smooth. Learn how to Trive with Arianna Huffington.
  • "BossyPants"
    Tiny Fey

    How did Tiny Fey get so funny? If you do not know, or if you don't think she is funny, this book is right up your alley. If you want to know more about Tiny Fey or little life hacks on how to live once you are famous, read BoosyPants.
    Do Not Recommend
  • "How to Become a Straight A Student"
    Cal Newport

    There are students out there who get straight A's and party all the time. How do they have the best of both worlds? Cal Newport, a professor at MIT has researched and interviewed many straight A students and wrote what their secrets to their successes are.
    Do Not Recommend
  • "Catching the Big Fish"
    David Lynch

    Meditation, Conciousness, and Creativity. There are little fish and big fish in the world, which one would you rather catch?
    Highly Do Not Recommend
  • "Ego is the Enemy"
    Ryan Holiday

    Ego is much more than thinking youre better than someone else. It is something everyone has to practice reducing everyday. One of the best books I read in 2020. Once you lose your ego, life begins to change for the better. Learn that ego will always sneak its way into your life, and how to stop it.
    Highly Recommend
  • "Set for Life"
    Scott Trench

    Retirement isn't just not working. Retirement is doing whatever you want on your own time. Set for Life teaches how anyone who is making a middle class income can become financially independent in 3 steps.
    Highly Recommend
  • "The Unfair Advantage"
    Ash Ali & Hasan Kubba

    Every business and every person has an unfair advantage. Ali and Hasan teaches the reader how to find theirs and how to use it for their gain.
    Do Not Recommend
  • "Get to the Point"
    Joel Schwartzberg

    What is worst than listening to a boring presentation, listening to a boring presentaion that has no point. Everytime you speak make sure you have a point to what you are saying and if you don't know how know you have a good point, this book is for you.
    Recommend
  • "Too Much and Never Enough"
    Mary Trump

    Why is Donald Trump the way he is, wether you like him or dislike him, this book will give you a small glimps on how his life was like before presidency.
    Highly Do Not Recommend
  • "The Most Good You Can Do"
    Peter Singer

    People always want to do good, it is evolutionarly bred into us. But what most people don't realize is there is mathmatically speaking, the best way to do good.
    Recommend
